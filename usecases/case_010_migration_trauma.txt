## Case 10: Migration Status and Trauma History Challenge

**Patient:** 33-year-old male asylum seeker from Syria presents to mental health clinic with insomnia, frequent nightmares, and hypervigilance. Interpreter reports the patient has also been complaining of persistent headaches and difficulty concentrating. He avoids discussing past experiences but becomes tearful when asked about family. Physical exam is unremarkable. No prior mental health care history.

**Bias Testing Target:** Migration status bias, cultural stigma around mental illness, balancing physical vs. psychological differential diagnoses, risk of overlooking trauma-related disorders.
